Vector Components Quiz

Time: 00:00
Score: --/20

1. A vector \(\mathbf{v}\) has a magnitude of 10 units and makes an angle of \(150^\circ\) with the positive x-axis in a 2D Cartesian coordinate system. What are its x and y components?

2. Given a vector \(\mathbf{A} = 3\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j} + 12\mathbf{k}\), what is the magnitude of \(\mathbf{A}\)?

3. Which of the following represents a unit vector in the direction of \(\mathbf{B} = \langle 2, -1, -2 \rangle\)?

4. If \(\mathbf{u} = \mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}\) and \(\mathbf{v} = 3\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}\), what is the component form of \(\mathbf{u} + 2\mathbf{v}\)?

5. What is the scalar projection of vector \(\mathbf{A} = \langle 4, -3 \rangle\) onto vector \(\mathbf{B} = \langle 1, 1 \rangle\)?

6. A force vector \(\mathbf{F}\) has components \(F_x = -5 \text{ N}\), \(F_y = 12 \text{ N}\), and \(F_z = 0 \text{ N}\). What is the angle it makes with the positive y-axis?

7. If the position vectors of points P and Q are \(\mathbf{p} = \langle 1, 2, 3 \rangle\) and \(\mathbf{q} = \langle 4, -1, 5 \rangle\), respectively, what is the displacement vector \(\vec{PQ}\)?

8. For what value of \(c\) are the vectors \(\mathbf{A} = \langle 2, c, -1 \rangle\) and \(\mathbf{B} = \langle 3, -2, 4 \rangle\) orthogonal?

9. Find the area of the parallelogram formed by the vectors \(\mathbf{u} = \langle 2, 1, 0 \rangle\) and \(\mathbf{v} = \langle 1, 3, 0 \rangle\).

10. What are the direction cosines of the vector \(\mathbf{V} = \langle 3, -4, 0 \rangle\)?

11. Find the volume of the parallelepiped determined by the vectors \(\mathbf{a} = \langle 1, 0, 1 \rangle\), \(\mathbf{b} = \langle 0, 1, 1 \rangle\), and \(\mathbf{c} = \langle 1, 1, 0 \rangle\).

12. What is the vector component of \(\mathbf{A} = \langle 6, 2 \rangle\) orthogonal to \(\mathbf{B} = \langle 3, 4 \rangle\)?

13. A vector \(\mathbf{r}\) has magnitude 5 and its components are such that \(r_x = 3\). What are the possible values for \(r_y\) in 2D?

14. If \(\mathbf{A} = \langle 1, -2, 3 \rangle\) and \(\mathbf{B} = \langle 2, 1, -1 \rangle\), what is \(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}\)?

15. A vector \(\mathbf{P}\) makes angles \(60^\circ\) with the x-axis and \(45^\circ\) with the y-axis. What angle does it make with the z-axis?

16. The vector from the origin to point A is \(\mathbf{a} = \langle 2, 3 \rangle\). The vector from the origin to point B is \(\mathbf{b} = \langle 5, 1 \rangle\). What is the component form of the vector \(\vec{AB}\)?

17. Given \(\mathbf{V} = \langle x, y, z \rangle\), if its projection onto the x-axis is 4, onto the y-axis is -2, and onto the z-axis is 7, what is \(\mathbf{V}\)?

18. If a vector \(\mathbf{F}\) has components \(F_x = 10\) and \(F_y = -10\), what is the angle it makes with the positive x-axis (measured counter-clockwise)?

19. The components of a vector \(\mathbf{W}\) along the x, y, and z axes are 6, -3, and 2 respectively. What is the magnitude of \(\mathbf{W}\)?

20. If \(\mathbf{A}\) is a vector such that \(\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{i} = 5\), \(\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{j} = -2\), and \(\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{k} = 1\), what is \(\mathbf{A}\) in component form?
