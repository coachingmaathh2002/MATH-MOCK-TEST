Applications of Vectors to Geometry Quiz

Time: 00:00

1. Given two vectors \(\mathbf{a} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ -3 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{b} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -2 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}\), find the area of the parallelogram spanned by \(\mathbf{a}\) and \(\mathbf{b}\).

2. What is the distance from the point \( P(1, 2, -1) \) to the plane \( 2x - 3y + 4z = 5 \)?

3. Find the equation of the plane that passes through the point \( (1, 0, -1) \) and is perpendicular to the line given by the parametric equations \( x = 1 + 2t, y = 3 - t, z = 4 + 3t \).

4. Calculate the volume of the parallelepiped with adjacent edges formed by the vectors \(\mathbf{u} = \mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}\), \(\mathbf{v} = 3\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}\), and \(\mathbf{w} = -\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j}\).

5. Determine the angle between the planes \( x + y - 2z = 5 \) and \( 2x - y + z = 1 \).

6. Find the projection of the vector \(\mathbf{a} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\) onto the vector \(\mathbf{b} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}\).

7. Two lines are given by \(\mathbf{r}_1(t) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} + t \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\mathbf{r}_2(s) = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 4 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} + s \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix}\). Determine if they intersect, and if so, find the point of intersection.

8. What is the vector equation of the line of intersection of the planes \( x + 2y - z = 1 \) and \( 2x - y + 3z = 4 \)?

9. A particle moves along a path defined by the vector function \(\mathbf{r}(t) = \cos(t)\mathbf{i} + \sin(t)\mathbf{j} + t\mathbf{k}\). Find the unit tangent vector to the path at \( t = \pi/2 \).

10. Find the distance between the skew lines \( L_1: \mathbf{r}(t) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} + t \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} \) and \( L_2: \mathbf{r}(s) = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} + s \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \).

11. What is the scalar projection of vector \(\mathbf{u} = \mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}\) onto vector \(\mathbf{v} = 3\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}\)?

12. Find the equation of the sphere with center \( (1, -2, 3) \) that is tangent to the plane \( 2x - y + z = 1 \).

13. Which of the following statements about the dot product and cross product of vectors is FALSE?

14. Find the parametric equations of the line passing through points \( A(2, -1, 3) \) and \( B(0, 4, -1) \).

15. What is the equation of the plane containing the points \( P(1, 0, 0) \), \( Q(0, 1, 0) \), and \( R(0, 0, 1) \)?

16. Given a vector \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -4 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix}\), find a unit vector in the opposite direction of \(\mathbf{v}\).

17. Find the point where the line \( \mathbf{r}(t) = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix} + t \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} \) intersects the plane \( x + y - z = 7 \).

18. If \(\mathbf{a}\) and \(\mathbf{b}\) are non-zero vectors, and \( \|\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b}\| = \|\mathbf{a}\| \|\mathbf{b}\| \), what can be concluded about the angle \(\theta\) between \(\mathbf{a}\) and \(\mathbf{b}\)?

19. Find the coordinates of the point \( P(2, 3, -1) \) reflected across the plane \( x + 2y - z = 0 \).

19. Find the coordinates of the point \( P(2, 3, -1) \) reflected across the plane \( x + 2y - z = 0 \).

20. The position vector of a particle is given by \( \mathbf{r}(t) = t^2 \mathbf{i} + (2t+1) \mathbf{j} + (t^3-t) \mathbf{k} \). Find the velocity vector at \( t = 1 \).
