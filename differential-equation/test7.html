Clairaut's Equation: General and Singular Solutions

1. Which of the following equations represents a Clairaut's equation?

2. What is the standard procedure to solve a Clairaut's equation \( y = xp + f(p) \)?

3. For the Clairaut's equation \( y = xp + f(p) \), differentiating with respect to \( x \) yields \( p = p + x\frac{dp}{dx} + f'(p)\frac{dp}{dx} \). What is the next step to find the solutions?

4. How is the general solution of a Clairaut's equation \( y = xp + f(p) \) obtained from \( (x + f'(p))\frac{dp}{dx} = 0 \)?

5. How is the singular solution of a Clairaut's equation \( y = xp + f(p) \) obtained?

6. Geometrically, what does the singular solution of a Clairaut's equation represent?

7. Find the general solution to the Clairaut's equation \( y = xp + p^2 \).

8. What is the singular solution for \( y = xp + p^2 \)?

9. Determine the general solution of \( y = xp + \ln p \).

10. Find the singular solution for \( y = xp + \ln p \).

11. For the equation \( y = xp + \sqrt{1+p^2} \), what is its general solution?

12. What is the singular solution for \( y = xp + \sqrt{1+p^2} \)?

13. The equation \( y = xp - p^3 \) has a general solution \( y = cx - c^3 \). What is its singular solution?

14. For a Clairaut's equation \( y = xp + f(p) \), a singular solution exists if and only if \( f''(p) \ne 0 \) (and can be expressed implicitly). Which of the following \( f(p) \) would NOT yield a singular solution?

15. If the general solution of a Clairaut's equation is a family of straight lines, what kind of curve is the singular solution?

16. Consider the Clairaut's equation \( y = xp + p \). What is its general solution?

17. Does the equation \( y = xp + p \) have a singular solution?

18. Which of the following is NOT a characteristic of a singular solution of a differential equation?

19. Consider the differential equation \( y = xp + \frac{1}{p} \). What is its singular solution?
