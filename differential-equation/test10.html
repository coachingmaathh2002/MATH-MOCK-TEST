Ordinary Differential Equations Quiz

Test your knowledge on the Order, Degree, and Formulation of ODEs.

1. What is the order and degree of the differential equation: \[ \frac{d^3y}{dx^3} + 5 \left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right)^2 - 4y = e^x \]

2. Determine the order and degree of the differential equation: \[ \left(\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}\right)^{3/2} = 5 \frac{dy}{dx} + y \]

3. What is the order and degree of the differential equation: \[ \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + \cos\left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right) = x^2 \]

4. Consider the differential equation: \[ \frac{d^4y}{dx^4} + \left(\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}\right)^5 + \frac{dy}{dx} = 0 \] What are its order and degree?

6. A population grows at a rate proportional to its current size. If \(P(t)\) is the population at time \(t\), which ODE models this scenario?

7. According to Newton's Law of Cooling, the rate of change of temperature of an object is proportional to the difference between its own temperature \(T\) and the ambient temperature \(T_a\). Which ODE represents this law?

8. A mass \(m\) is attached to a spring with spring constant \(k\). Assuming no damping and no external forces, the displacement \(x(t)\) from equilibrium follows simple harmonic motion. Which ODE describes this motion?

9. What is the degree of the differential equation: \[ \sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right)^2} = \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} \]

10. The rate at which a substance decays is proportional to the amount of substance present. If \(A(t)\) is the amount of substance at time \(t\), which ODE describes this?

11. What is the order of the differential equation: \[ y^{(4)} - 2y'' + y' - 7y = \sin(x) \]

12. An object falls under gravity with air resistance proportional to its velocity. If \(v(t)\) is its velocity and \(g\) is acceleration due to gravity, which ODE models its motion? (Assume upward is positive, so gravity is \(-g\)).

13. What is the degree of the differential equation: \[ y'' = \sin(x) \left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right)^2 + e^y \]

14. Formulate the ODE for a tank containing 100 liters of water with 10 kg of salt dissolved. Brine containing 0.5 kg/L of salt enters the tank at 5 L/min. The mixture leaves at the same rate. Let \(Q(t)\) be the amount of salt in the tank at time \(t\).

15. The equation for a series RLC circuit with voltage source \(E(t)\) is given by: \[ L \frac{d^2q}{dt^2} + R \frac{dq}{dt} + \frac{1}{C} q = E(t) \] What are the order and degree of this ODE?

16. What is the order and degree of the differential equation: \[ \left( \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} \right)^2 + \frac{dy}{dx} + y = 0 \]

17. Formulate the ODE for a simple pendulum of length \(L\) with mass \(m\), ignoring air resistance. Let \(\theta(t)\) be the angular displacement from the vertical. (Hint: Use Newton's second law for rotational motion).

18. What is the order and degree of the differential equation: \[ \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = \sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right)^3} \]

19. Formulate the ODE for a substance that dissolves at a rate proportional to the product of the amount of undissolved substance \(S(t)\) and the difference between the saturation concentration \(C_s\) and the current concentration \(C(t)\). Assume volume \(V\) is constant.

20. What are the order and degree of the differential equation: \[ \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + y = \frac{1}{\frac{dy}{dx}} \]
